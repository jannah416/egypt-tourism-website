Welcome to Egypt

Egypt is a country rich in history, culture, and breathtaking landscapes. Its most iconic destination is the Pyramids of Giza, a testament to the ancient Egyptians' architectural genius. Cairo, the bustling capital, is home to the impressive Egyptian Museum, housing thousands of ancient artifacts, including the treasures of King Tutankhamun. Luxor, often called the world’s greatest open-air museum, offers visitors the awe-inspiring Karnak Temple and the Valley of the Kings, where pharaohs were laid to rest. For those seeking relaxation, the Red Sea resorts of Hurghada and Sharm El Sheikh offer stunning coral reefs, making Egypt a premier destination for snorkeling and diving. Whether exploring the wonders of ancient civilizations or enjoying the natural beauty of the desert and sea, Egypt offers a diverse range of attractions

Luxor

Egypt’s Open-Air Museum

Soak in the sunshine, sink into glorious sunsets, and sail back in time. Luxor, Egypt’s open-air museum, holds the memories and chronicles of ancient Egyptian gods and royalty. Split into two halves, the East Bank and West Bank, by the River Nile, Luxor never fails to mesmerize its visitors. Be prepared to wander through the hallowed halls of time and feel very small standing in the shadows of this city’s massive painted columns, statues, and walls decorated with beautiful reliefs.

What you need to see

East Bank

Stroll through the halls and courtyards of Luxor and Karnak Temples, the most revered sites in Luxor.

West Bank

Ferry from the land of the living on the East Bank to the holy burial grounds on the West Bank.

Dicover Hidden Gems

Watch an unforgettable sunrise from a hot air balloon.

Catch the sunset from a rooftop hotel restaurant overlooking Luxor Temple.

Take a ride on a horse drawn carriage known as the caleche or hantour, Luxor’s version of a taxi.

Cairo

From ancient wonders to modern neighborhoods, discover Egypt’s beating heart

Cairo is the capital of Egypt and one of the world’s largest megacities, with a vast metropolitan area stretching out as Greater Cairo to include Giza and the outer suburbs that have developed to the east and west of the city center. The beating heart of the city is nestled and centered around the banks of Egypt’s majestic River Nile and its glistening waters that have served as a lifeline for Egyptians since the dawn of human civilization.

What you need to see

Historic Cairo

Witness the richness of the city’s Islamic and Coptic historical sites, marveling at the “City of a Thousand Minarets.”

Downtown Cairo & City Breaks

Visit the true soul of Cairo and hit the streets of its contemporary, upscale neighborhoods.

Dicover Hidden Gems

Catch an authentic whirling dervishes performance at Wikalat al-Ghuri.

Sharm el-Sheikh

The Red Sea’s charming city of peace

World-renowned for the picturesque marine life that thrives under its turquoise blue waters, Sharm al-Sheikh is a captivating Egyptian Red Sea resort city nestled between Ras Muhammad National Park and the Nabq Protectorate. “Sharm”, as it’s referred to by Egyptians, first appeared on the tourist map in the 1980’s, but back then, it was mostly visited by dive enthusiasts who were captivated by its magnificent coral reefs and virgin beaches.

What you need to see

Beaches, Snorkeling & Diving

Sunbathe under clear blue skies or dive into the sea and marvel at the kaleidoscope of marine life underneath the water.

Adventure & Activities

Play a leisurely game of golf, go soaring across sand dunes, or get a birds-eye view of the bays from a parachute.

Dicover Hidden Gems

Take a boat trip to White Island off the coast of Ras Muhammad—it’s only visible during low tide.

Go on a spiritual journey with a sunrise trek up to the top of Mount St Catherineit’s only two and a half hours away by car or bus.

Head to the Old Market and shop for local souvenirs and spices you didn’t even know existed.
